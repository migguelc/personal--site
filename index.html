Hey!

I’m Miguel, a systems engineer and builder based in Porto. I work where strategy, product and artificial intelligence meet.

I approach problems as systems: relationships, root causes and downstream effects come before tools. Then I build the simplest solution capable of changing the outcome.

/ 00 · way of thinking

Before adding compute, add understanding. No amount of technology can fix a problem you do not understand. The goal is not to use AI. It is to build the smallest system that changes the outcome.

The path so far

Six moments. Each one shaped a way of thinking that is still present in the work.

01 ·2023
The first yes

Stepping into the unfamiliar.

At 17, a teacher invited me to join my school’s robotics and programming group. I knew little about either. I said yes anyway. That decision taught me something I still carry: growth begins beyond what we already know.

02 ·2023
FNR & RoboCup

Learning to build within constraints.

Our team won the OnStage category at Portugal’s National Robotics Festival and represented the country at RoboCup 2023, finishing 11th worldwide. Working with a fraction of other teams’ resources taught us to iterate faster, simplify harder and avoid buying our way around problems. Competing abroad also taught me to collaborate across cultures and adapt quickly in an unfamiliar environment.

03 ·2023–2026
Systems Engineering

Learning to see the whole.

At ISEP, I found the discipline that best reflects how I think: understand relationships, trade-offs and downstream effects before optimising individual parts. Systems Engineering did not define what I wanted to become. It gave me a lens through which to approach almost anything.

systems thinkingengineeringdecisions

04 ·2023
Entrepreneurship

Finding an ecosystem, not just a classroom.

I began my entrepreneurial journey as soon as I entered university. I quickly realised I had found more than an education: an ecosystem of opportunities, people and connections. My first project was ISION, an IoT concept for smart cities built with LoRaWAN. We placed third in Poliempreende Porto, turning an early idea into my first real lesson in entrepreneurship.

05 ·2026

Making strategy operational.

Kind Tech was my first professional experience. As Head of Growth, I worked across brand, strategy and product. I helped shape the startup’s identity and created Kind OS, an internal operating system connecting commercial strategy, planning and sprint execution. It taught me that strategy only matters when it changes how people work.

Choosing to build.

Together with two other systems thinkers, I chose to risk the safety of employment and turn this vision into a services startup: go to the root of the problem, think in systems and use AI to make that combination extraordinarily powerful. Principles

001

002

003

If the path does not demand growth, it is probably not the path I am looking for.
